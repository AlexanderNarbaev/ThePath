## For all community members, mentors, civil servants, and everyone who strives to live by the principles of the Spiral of Consciousness

---

### Introduction

The three absolute prohibitions — do not kill, do not torture, do not enslave — are red lines. Crossing them destroys cooperation and leads to the disintegration of the community. But an ethics founded solely on prohibitions is not enough. We cannot simply "not do bad" — we must strive toward something. Otherwise, the system remains empty, without orientation, without inspiration.

The four principles of Flourishing give us direction. They do not prescribe exactly what to do in every situation, but they set a vector: develop yourself, create and help, think for the long-term future, preserve and multiply consciousness.

This module is a practical guide to applying ethics in daily life, in professional activity, and in public service. It shows how to train virtues, resolve dilemmas, evaluate the consequences of one's actions, and — critically — how to maintain ethical integrity while wielding power or force.

## Section 1. From the Three Prohibitions to a Full Ethics

The three prohibitions are the **minimum framework**. They are necessary, but not sufficient. A community where no one kills, tortures, or enslaves can still be:
- Apathetic (no one develops).
- Selfish (no one helps).
- Short-sighted (no one thinks about the future).
- Degrading (attention is scattered on digital garbage).

Therefore, we add **the four principles of Flourishing** — guideposts that indicate where to move.

| Principle | Short Formula | What It Prohibits (or Limits) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Develop Yourself | Become better every day | Stagnation, laziness, refusal to learn |
| Create and Help | Add value to the world | Parasitism, destruction, indifference |
| Think for the Long-Term Future | Plan for 100, 1,000, 1,000,000 years | Short-term gain, resource exploitation |
| Preserve and Multiply Consciousness | Guard attention, deepen perception | Degradation of attention, social media addiction, clip thinking |

These principles are not "commandments" with punishment for non-compliance. They are ethical guideposts. One may fall short of them without being expelled from the community. But following them makes life brighter, more meaningful, and more resilient.

## Section 2. The Four Principles of Flourishing — Detailed Breakdown

### 2.1. Develop Yourself

**What does this mean?**
Developing yourself is not only intellectual growth (reading books, learning languages). It also includes:
- Emotional intelligence (understanding your own and others' feelings).
- Cognitive resilience (the ability to concentrate despite noise).
- Attention skills (control over digital habits).
- Physical health (exercise, sleep, nutrition).
- Social skills (the ability to listen, conduct dialogue, resolve conflicts).

### 2.2. Create and Help

**What does this mean?**
Creativity is not only art. It is any action that adds value to the world. Helping others must not harm yourself. The principle of "put your own oxygen mask on first, then the child's."

### 2.3. Think for the Long-Term Future

**What does this mean?**
Every decision we make affects not only us but also future generations. The planning horizon is at least 100 years, ideally — 1,000, 10,000, 1,000,000 years. This does not mean we must forget today's needs. But we must avoid actions that foreclose the future.

### 2.4. Preserve and Multiply Consciousness

**What does this mean?**
The Era of Trial is characterized by the degradation of attention. Social media algorithms, endless notifications, short videos, clickbait — all of this destroys the capacity for deep perception. To preserve consciousness means to consciously limit digital garbage, cultivate attention, and practice reflection.

## Section 3. The Ethics of Power and Force

This section is addressed to those who, by the duty of their office, are vested with the right to apply coercion: police officers, military personnel, judges, corrections officers, leaders. Wielding force is not a privilege but the greatest ethical trial. The Spiral recognizes the necessity of these professions for the protection of order and life, but demands strict limitations.

### 3.1. Strict Boundaries for the Use of Force

Force is permissible only in three cases, and in no others:

1.  **Defense of life.** Preventing a direct, imminent, proven threat to life — one's own or another's. Defense of property, abstract "state interests," or "uniform honor" is not grounds for the use of force.
2.  **Stopping violence.** Halting an act of violence occurring here and now.
3.  **Enforcement of a lawful decision.** Carrying out a court decision rendered within a transparent and fair procedure against a defendant found guilty.

**Force is never used for:**
- Revenge.
- Intimidation or punishment without trial.
- Suppression of peaceful protest or dissent.
- Obtaining information (this violates the second prohibition — torture).

### 3.2. The Criminal Order

Any order that directly or indirectly requires the executor to violate one of the three prohibitions — to torture, to kill without a direct threat, to enslave — is **criminal and inherently void**.

A follower of the Spiral in public service **is obligated to refuse to carry out such an order**. This is not treason and not a breach of subordination. It is loyalty to a higher ethical law that stands above the orders of superiors and the decrees of governments.

Refusal of a criminal order is an act of the highest courage. The Spiral community is obligated to support and protect the one who has taken such a step.

### 3.3. The Ethics of the Judge

A judge who shares the principles of the Spiral must:
- **Be impartial.** Justice does not depend on the status, origin, or beliefs of the defendant.
- **Uphold the presumption of innocence.** No one may be punished until their guilt is proven within a transparent procedure.
- **Ensure proportionality of punishment.** The punishment must correspond to the gravity of the harm and be directed at restoration and re-education, not retribution.

## Section 4. The "Dirty Hands" Dilemma — and Why the Spiral Rejects It

In political philosophy, there is the concept of "dirty hands": a leader is sometimes *obligated* to commit an immoral act (for example, to torture a terrorist to learn where a bomb is planted) in order to save many lives, and he bears this sin on his conscience personally.

The Spiral considers this logic destructive and categorically rejects it. Why?

1.  **Consequences are unpredictable.** We never know for certain whether torture will save lives or lead to even greater casualties in the future (for example, through the radicalization of new terrorists).
2.  **The institution of trust is destroyed.** If the state or community reserves the right "in an extreme case" to torture, this undermines everyone's trust in everyone. No one can be certain that the "extreme case" will not befall them tomorrow.
3.  **A precedent is created.** A single exception made "for the higher good" becomes the justification for thousands of subsequent abuses. The boundary between the "extreme case" and everyday practice erodes very quickly.

The three prohibitions are absolute precisely because any violation of them, even with the best intentions, destroys the long-term cooperation on which the entire edifice of the Spiral rests. This is not weakness, but the highest ethical rigor.

## Section 5. Controversial Issues: The Spiral's Position

### 5.1. The Death Penalty

The death penalty is **impermissible**.
- It violates the first prohibition (killing). A convicted person in custody does not pose a direct and imminent threat. The threat from them is potential, not direct.
- The judicial system is prone to error. A death sentence is irreversible. The Spiral cannot endorse a punishment that cannot be corrected in the event of a judicial error.

### 5.2. Euthanasia

Euthanasia is **permissible under the strictest conditions**:
- An incurable nature of the illness is confirmed, causing unbearable physical suffering that cannot be alleviated with available means.
- There exists a clear, repeatedly expressed, and documented will of the patient in sound mind and firm memory.
- The decision is confirmed by an independent medical commission that includes not only physicians but also ethics specialists.
- The procedure is carried out in the most painless and humane manner.

The Spiral does not encourage euthanasia, but respects the individual's right to relief from meaningless suffering in a hopeless situation.

### 5.3. Abortion

This is the most complex and internally conflicting issue.
- **The Spiral recognizes a woman's right to autonomy over her own body.** Up to 12 weeks, the decision on abortion is the exclusive right of the woman.
- **After 12 weeks, abortion is permissible only on medical grounds**, when the pregnancy threatens the life or health of the mother, or in the case of detection of severe fetal pathologies incompatible with life.
- **The Spiral does not encourage abortion as a means of contraception**, but neither does it stigmatize those who have made this choice. The community must create conditions to support motherhood and childhood so that economic and social reasons are not the sole grounds for terminating a pregnancy.
- **This position is a compromise.** Communities may have stricter ethical norms, but they may not be imposed on all members.

## Section 6. Table of Virtues and Vices

Virtues are skills. They can be trained. The table below presents key virtues, their opposing vices, and methods of training.

| Virtue | Definition | Vice (Deficiency) | Vice (Excess) | How to Train |
|--------|------------|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Courage** | The ability to act despite fear, for higher purposes | Cowardice | Recklessness | Gradually step out of the comfort zone, speak the truth in difficult situations |
| **Temperance** | Control of desires without their complete suppression | Asceticism (denial of needs) | Dissoluteness | Digital fast, giving up excesses for a week |
| **Generosity** | Readiness to share resources (time, money, attention) | Stinginess | Profligacy | Donate 5–10% of surplus income to the community fund |
| **Truthfulness** | Speaking the truth, but with tact and goodwill | Deceitfulness | Tactlessness ("brutal honesty") | Evening reflection: "Where did I lie or exaggerate today?" |
| **Justice** | Rendering to each their due, but with mercy | Vindictiveness | Universal forgiveness (absence of sanctions) | Participation in Reconciliation Circles (Module 5) |
| **Friendliness** | Being pleasant in communication, avoiding excessive conflict | Reclusiveness (avoiding people) | Obsequiousness (to one's own detriment) | Regular Gatherings of Reason, practice of active listening |
| **Humility** | Knowing one's limits, being open to criticism | Pride (inability to learn) | Self-abasement (refusal of opportunities) | The Hour of Acknowledged Ignorance (Module 3) |

## Section 7. Code of Honor for Three Levels of Affiliation

### Sympathizer
- I share the two axioms of the Spiral and accept the three prohibitions as my personal moral law.
- I strive to follow the four principles of Flourishing to the best of my ability.
- I practice 3 minutes of attention daily, when I can.
- I do not dishonor the Spiral with actions that clearly contradict its spirit.

### Follower
- I observe everything prescribed for a Sympathizer, unswervingly.
- I have publicly adopted the Creed and I am aware of my responsibility to the community.
- I actively train virtues and participate in Gatherings of Reason.
- I do not make empty promises and always strive to fulfill the commitments I undertake.
- I do not use affiliation with the Spiral for personal gain or power.

### Keeper of Consciousness
- I observe everything prescribed for a Follower, with maximum rigor.
- I have taken deep vows (a daily hour of practice, a monthly fast, an annual retreat, mentorship).
- I serve as an example to others, but do not demand special treatment. My authority lies only in my knowledge and deeds, not in formal status.
- I teach newcomers, but do not impose my path on them. I am a gardener who creates conditions for growth, not a sculptor who imposes a shape.
- I remember: the power of a Keeper is power over oneself, not over others.

## Section 8. Scale of Consequences and Exclusion Procedure

### 8.1. Scale of Consequences

| Level | Violation | Example | Community Response |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **0** | Unconscious error | Forgot a small promise | Private apology |
| **1** | Minor, conscious | A harsh word in an argument | Private conversation, apologies, compensation |
| **2** | Medium, repeated | Systematic lying, appropriation of shared resources | Public remark, compensation, temporary suspension of voting rights (1–3 months) |
| **3** | Grave, without violating the three prohibitions | Sabotage of shared projects, slander | Exclusion from operational circles, suspension of voting rights for one year, mandatory Reconciliation Circle |
| **4** | Violation of the three prohibitions | Murder, torture, slavery | **Immediate exclusion from the community, referral to state law enforcement agencies** |

### 8.2. Procedure for Exclusion from the Community

Exclusion is the termination of membership and all associated rights.

**Initiation:** a decision to exclude for a Level 4 violation may be initiated by any member of the community or by the council.
**Proceedings:**
1.  If the violation is obvious (proven or admitted), the community council convenes an emergency session within 24 hours and issues a decision on exclusion (requires a 2/3 majority of the council).
2.  If there are doubts, an urgent general assembly is convened, where testimony is heard, and the decision on exclusion is taken by a 2/3 majority of the members present.
**Guarantees:** the person being excluded is given the floor for their defense. The decision may be appealed to the community civil assembly or external arbitration.
**Consequences:** the excluded person is immediately stripped of all rights and insignia. The community may not apply violence against them, but is obligated to transmit information about the crime to state authorities.

## Section 9. Ethics for Different Roles

### For the Mentor
**Prohibited:** using authority for personal gain, entering into intimate relations with mentees, demanding personal gratitude, disclosing confidential information without consent.
**Recommended:** regular supervision, attending to one's own burnout (Module 9).

### For the Community Coordinator
**Prohibited:** non-transparent financial management, nepotism, ignoring complaints.
**Recommended:** quarterly reports, rotation of roles (no more than 1–2 years).

### For the Civil Servant
**Prohibited:** carrying out criminal orders, using official position for personal purposes, concealing information about violations of the three prohibitions.
**Duty:** if a servant has witnessed systematic violations of the three prohibitions in their agency, they are obligated to report this through available legal channels (up to and including public disclosure, if internal mechanisms do not work).

## Section 10. Ethical Self-Assessment Checklist

Use this checklist for weekly reflection (ideally on Sunday evening).

- [ ] **Did I violate the three prohibitions today?** (Did I not kill, not torture, not enslave — even in a figurative sense?)
- [ ] **Did I perform at least one action for my own development?** (Read something, took a lesson, trained my attention?)
- [ ] **Did I help someone (without harming myself)?** (Gave advice, helped physically, donated?)
- [ ] **Did I think about the long-term consequences of my actions?** (Did I not harm future generations?)
- [ ] **Did I preserve my attention from digital garbage?** (Practiced the digital shield, limited social media?)
- [ ] **Did I manifest at least one virtue?** (Courage, temperance, generosity, truthfulness, justice, friendliness, humility?)
- [ ] **Did I abuse my power or influence?** (This item is mandatory for Keepers and leaders.)

**If you answered "no" to the majority of the questions — this is not a reason for guilt. It is a reason to think about how to adjust the coming week.**
