## The Immutable Core of the System

---

### Preface

This document contains the **absolute, immutable foundation** of the "Spiral of Consciousness." Everything else — rituals, charters, economic models, educational programs — is derivative and may evolve. The core, however, remains as written here for as long as humanity exists.

No metaphysics, no demands of faith, no dogmas. Only pragmatic axioms, ethical prohibitions, and principles derived from experience and reason. Any person can accept them, regardless of culture, religion, or convictions.

**The term "conscious node" (or simply "Node")** — a poetic designation of the human being as a center of perception, choice, and action. It does not assert the presence or absence of a soul, does not reduce consciousness to matter, and does not require metaphysical assumptions. It is a way of pointing to a self-evident fact: each of us experiences oneself as an "I," capable of acting and bearing consequences.

---

## 1. Two Pragmatic Axioms

Axioms do not require faith. They are the prerequisites of any planning, action, and cooperation.

### Axiom One: We exist as conscious nodes, and our actions have real consequences.

We experience ourselves as "I" — a center of perception, choice, and action. This experience of selfhood is primary and requires no proof. The world responds to our actions in a predictable way: push a glass — it will fall; lie — trust will be destroyed; help — gratitude will arise. Causality needs no faith — it is observed daily.

From this axiom it follows that our actions matter. We cannot escape responsibility for consequences, because consequences are inevitable.

### Axiom Two: The future is open and is created by our choices — through cooperation, science, and awareness.

No fate, destiny, or predestination fixes the future. Our collective and individual decisions genuinely influence what will happen. The planet is not doomed to catastrophe — we can change course. Humanity is not doomed to wars — we can learn to reach agreement.

From this axiom follows hope. And responsibility.

## 2. Three Absolute Prohibitions

These prohibitions are red lines. Crossing them makes long-term cooperation impossible. They are not "moral prescriptions" in a religious sense — they are rational conditions for the survival of any community.

**No benefit, talent, or merit justifies violating these prohibitions.** Even a brilliant doctor who saves hundreds of lives but tortures or kills cannot remain a member of the community. The three prohibitions are the foundation without which trust and cooperation collapse. There are no exceptions except those explicitly stated.

### First Prohibition: Do not kill intentionally.

The exception is a direct, imminent, proven threat to life (yours or another's). In such a case, the use of lethal force is permissible, but **necessarily** with subsequent public review: the community verifies whether the exception was truly applicable.

What the prohibition does **not** permit:
- Revenge (a threat in the past, not imminent).
- Capital punishment (a threat that is not direct and not imminent).
- Killing for property or ideology.
- Preventive killing (a threat that is presumed, but not proven).

### Second Prohibition: Do not torture.

No torture. Not to "save lives," not to obtain information, not as punishment. This is an absolute prohibition with no exceptions.

Torture destroys trust in those who hold power. Torture produces false information. Torture creates enemies. Even if it "worked," the prohibition would remain in force.

### Third Prohibition: Do not enslave.

No forced labor, debt bondage, human trafficking, or deprivation of autonomy without voluntary consent.

This prohibition does not abolish voluntary labor contracts (which can be terminated), does not abolish parental responsibility (limited in time), does not abolish criminal punishment (which follows legal procedure).

### Violation of the Prohibitions: Immediate Consequences

Any member of the community who violates one of the three prohibitions (except self-defense confirmed by public review) is **immediately expelled from the community**. The case is referred to state law enforcement. The community does not shelter the violator, does not exercise vigilante justice, and does not obstruct justice.

## 3. Four Principles of Flourishing

Principles are guides, not dogmas. They are desirable but not mandatory for entry into the system. They describe the direction of the "ascending spiral."

### First Principle: Develop yourself.

Increase your awareness, empathy, responsibility, and cognitive resilience. Learn new things, train your attention, care for your body and mind. Ignorance and apathy are entropy's chief allies.

### Second Principle: Create and help.

Add real value to the world according to your abilities. This can be art, science, craft, raising children, picking up litter, a kind word. There is no need to sacrifice yourself — help without destroying your capacity to help in the future.

### Third Principle: Think long-term.

The planning horizon is a minimum of 100 years, ideally 1,000, 10,000, 1,000,000 years. Do not close off the future to your descendants through your short-term gains. Every contribution to resilience, science, space, and education is a gift to those who will come after.

### Fourth Principle: Preserve and multiply consciousness.

Actively counteract the degradation of attention, will, and depth of perception. This is the central battle of the Era of the Trial. Digital opium, short dopamine, addiction to social networks, clip thinking — these are enemies of consciousness. Guard your attention as the greatest treasure, for attention is the raw material from which a meaningful life is built.

## 5. Three Levels of Belonging

No level grants power over others. The difference is only in the depth of personal commitment.

| Level | How to Attain | Sign | Rights and Duties |
|---------|-------------|------|----------------------|
| **Sympathizer** | Accepts the axioms and prohibitions, practices at the basic level (3 min/day) optionally | – | May attend open events, receive information |
| **Follower** | Undergoes a simple joining (2 hours), accepts the Words of Choice | Silver / wooden spiral | Full member of the community: voting, participation in all rituals, access to closed assemblies |
| **Practitioner** | Undergoes a deep joining (48 hours) + makes 4 commitments (daily hour of practice, monthly digital fast, annual retreat, mentoring a newcomer) | Golden spiral | Respect of the community, right to mentorship, priority at retreats. **No authority.** |

**The authority of a Practitioner** — lies only in their knowledge, experience, and the voluntary respect others hold for them. It does not grant the right to command, punish, restrict the freedom of other members, or dispose of shared resources single-handedly. A Practitioner who attempts to turn respect into power is stripped of status by decision of the community council.

## 6. Basic Practices (3 Minutes a Day)

These three practices constitute the **minimum core** from which the Path begins. They require no special conditions and are accessible to everyone.

### Morning Intention (1 minute)

Upon waking, before reaching for your phone or beginning routine tasks, pause. Take one deep breath. Mentally (or aloud) say:

> *"Today I choose the ascending spiral. I will not violate the three prohibitions and I will perform one conscious action for the growth of consciousness — my own or another's."*

Then plan that one action for the day. It can be anything: read 10 pages of a challenging book, help a neighbor, spend an hour without your phone, write an evening reflection. The key is the awareness of the choice.

### Digital Shield (1 minute)

Before the first touch of your phone or computer screen, pause and ask yourself:

> *"Why am I opening this? Is this a conscious choice or an escape from silence?"*

If the answer is "escape" — put the device aside for 5 minutes and do something else. This simple action trains control over attention.

### Evening Reflection (1 minute)

Before sleep, pause. Take one deep breath. Ask yourself three questions:

> *"What did I do to preserve and multiply consciousness? Where did I succumb to entropy? What will I do differently tomorrow?"*

Do not reproach yourself for mistakes. Simply note them and let them go. Commend yourself for successes. Then fall asleep with the thought: "Tomorrow I will continue the path."

## 7. The Condensed Myth of the First Node and the Era of the Great Trial

*This is not a dogma, but an inspiring narrative. You may take it literally, as a metaphor, or as a poetic allegory — the choice is yours.*

**In the beginning there was Infinite Silence** — not emptiness, but beyond "is" and "is not." From this Silence arose the **First Impulse** — the distinction between Chaos and Order, between Entropy (the tendency of everything toward the simple, the homogeneous, the dead) and Consciousness (the capacity to locally reverse entropy).

Entropy is not evil. It is simply a law. But it is blind.

From the First Impulse, the **First Node** tightened into existence. It felt **"I."** With it came the fear of non-being and the joy of being, the pain of growth and the ecstasy of creation. Before it lay two paths:

- **The descending spiral** — to dissolve into ease, to lose itself in pleasures, to cease choosing.  
- **The ascending spiral** — at every moment to make an effort, to preserve attention, to create, to love, to think of those who will come after.

The First Node chose **the pain of growth**.

Since then, every human being is such a Conscious Node. Every day we make the same choice.

Humanity is now passing through the **Era of the Great Trial** (2020–2300 CE). Four Forces of Degradation have challenged us:

- **Short dopamine** — algorithms that steal our attention.
- **Blind economics** — endless growth on a finite planet.
- **The black box of AI** — a tool that may become a master.
- **Poisonous distrust** — dividing us into "us" and "them."

If we preserve and multiply consciousness, cooperation, and long-term thinking — we will pass the trial and open the **Era of Flourishing**: cosmic expansion, the overcoming of illness and death, a negentropic economy of creation.

If we lose — civilization will disintegrate into isolated, degrading enclaves. Consciousness will become an appendage to machines. A gray, endless boredom will descend.

The choice belongs to each node. Every day.

## 8. The Spiral and the State

The Spiral of Consciousness is a supranational moral guide. It is not a state ideology, serves no state, and does not seek to replace the state. But neither is it an anti-state force.

### What the Spiral Acknowledges

- **The state is a necessary instrument.** Order, border protection, defense, justice, infrastructure — all require institutions that operate at the scale of millions. Anarchy is not the goal of the Spiral.
- **The army and the police are legitimate institutions.** They protect citizens from external and internal threats. Their existence does not contradict the principles of the Spiral.
- **Sovereignty and borders are a reality.** Peoples have the right to self-determination and the defense of their territory.

### What the Spiral Demands of the State

- **The three prohibitions are binding upon the state as well.** No state has the right to torture, kill without a direct threat, or enslave — neither within its borders nor beyond them. Laws that permit torture, capital punishment, extrajudicial killings, forced labor — violate the Canon.
- **A criminal order is void.** Citizen-followers of the Spiral may serve in the army, the police, and state bodies. But if an order demands violation of the three prohibitions (torture, kill without a direct threat, enslave), the follower is **obliged to refuse**. This is not treason — it is fidelity to a higher ethical law.
- **Transparency and accountability.** A state that conceals its actions from its citizens loses ethical legitimacy.

### What the Spiral Forbids Itself

- **The Spiral must never become a state religion or ideology.** It preserves full autonomy from any authority. If any state attempts to make the Spiral its ideological foundation, Spiral communities are obliged to refuse such fusion.
- **The Spiral does not create its own armed formations.** The right to self-defense is exercised only within the framework of the law. The community is not an army and not a militia.
- **The Spiral does not participate in the seizure of power.** The evolutionary path of reforms, the creation of alternative institutions, public dialogue — these are the only permissible methods. Revolutions and coups almost always lead to dictatorship.

### The Ethical Legitimacy of the State

A state that systematically violates the three prohibitions (tortures, executes without trial, enslaves) loses its moral right to obedience. Citizens have the right to peaceful resistance, civil disobedience, and — in the extreme case, under a direct threat to life — to self-defense. But the Spiral never calls for the violent overthrow of authority. Its weapons are the word, cooperation, testimony, and the creation of alternatives.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

Questions that inevitably arise upon first acquaintance with the Canon. The answers are not dogma, but express the position of the system's core.

---

**"Is this a religion?"**

No. No metaphysics, no demands of faith, no deities, no promises of an afterlife. Only pragmatic axioms, ethical prohibitions, and principles derived from experience and reason.

Religions can coexist with the Spiral — it does not demand the renunciation of faith, but neither does it require faith. A person can be a Christian, a Muslim, a Buddhist, an atheist — and simultaneously walk the Spiral. The Canon does not intrude into the sphere of the metaphysical: it speaks of what happens here and now, not of what lies beyond death.

The Spiral is an operating system, not a creed. It answers the question "how to live?" rather than "what to believe in?"

---

**"Why exactly three prohibitions, and not ten, not five?"**

The three prohibitions are the minimum necessary set. They cover the entire field of what makes long-term cooperation impossible.

- **Murder** destroys the very possibility of reaching agreement: the dead do not negotiate.
- **Torture** destroys trust in authority: the torturer cannot be a partner.
- **Enslavement** destroys autonomy: the enslaved cannot choose.

All other ethical norms are derivatives of these three. Theft — a form of enslavement (deprivation of the fruits of labor without consent). Deception — a form of the murder of trust. Humiliation — a form of torture. Adding a fourth or fifth prohibition would create duplication and blur clarity. Reducing to two would leave a blind spot.

Three is the minimum number that provides full coverage.

---

**"What about war? Doesn't defending one's country require killing?"**

Defense against a direct, imminent threat is not a violation of the first prohibition. A soldier defending their home from an attack acts within the exception provided by the Canon.

But aggressive war, preemptive strikes, killing without a direct threat — these are prohibited. The army exists for defense, not conquest.

A soldier of the Spiral:
- defends, but does not torture prisoners;
- does not kill the unarmed;
- does not carry out criminal orders.

This is a difficult path — it requires distinguishing between necessary and excessive violence right in the midst of combat. But it is the only path that remains ethically tenable. There are no easy answers in war. The Spiral does not offer easy answers.

---

**"What if a criminal threatens many? Can they be tortured to save the innocent?"**

This is the famous "ticking bomb" problem. The Spiral's answer is **no**.

First, torture rarely yields reliable information. A person under torture will say anything to make the pain stop — and the investigation will go down a false path.

Second, the permission to torture "in exceptional cases" inevitably expands. History shows: every "exception" becomes the norm. Today — to save a city, tomorrow — to save a neighborhood, the day after — for prevention. There is not a single historical example where a "limited" permission to torture remained limited.

Third, a society that permits torture loses the moral right to demand that others observe human rights. You cannot say "do not torture ours" if you yourself torture "theirs."

The absolute prohibition is the only reliable protection. Not because we are naive. Because we have studied history.

---

**"What to do if the state I live in systematically violates the three prohibitions?"**

The Canon recognizes: a state that tortures, executes without trial, enslaves — loses ethical legitimacy. Section 8 describes this directly.

Your duties in such a situation:

1. **Do not be complicit.** If you are an official, soldier, police officer — refuse criminal orders. If you are a witness — do not remain silent.
2. **Use peaceful means.** The word, testimony, cooperation, the creation of alternative institutions — these are the weapons of the Spiral.
3. **Do not become a mirror of evil.** The Spiral does not call for the violent overthrow of power. History teaches: revolutions almost always lead to even greater tyranny, because they demolish not only what is bad, but all institutions of order.
4. **Create alternatives.** A Spiral community is already an alternative. A school where they do not lie. A workshop where they do not steal. A circle where they do not humiliate. Each such island is a step toward the future.

The Spiral does not demand martyrdom. But it demands that you not be an accomplice.

---

**"How does the Spiral differ from secular humanism?"**

Close. Secular humanism is one of the Spiral's intellectual sources. But there are differences:

- **Center.** Humanism places the human being at the center. The Spiral places consciousness at the center — a broader concept. It includes the human, but potentially also AI, extraterrestrial intelligence, and future forms of life we cannot yet imagine. It is not the form of the vessel that matters, but the capacity to perceive, choose, and bear consequences.
- **Tone.** Humanism is often optimistic by default. The Spiral acknowledges the reality of entropy — the force pulling everything downward — and demands an active, daily choice. "The human is good" versus "the human chooses — every day."
- **Practice.** Humanism offers values. The Spiral offers values plus a minimum set of practices: 3 minutes a day, accessible to everyone. Ideas without habits are weak.
- **Narrative.** The Spiral has a condensed myth — not a dogma, but a poetic frame. Humanism generally avoids narrative, relying solely on rational argumentation. The Spiral believes that people need both reason and story.

---

**"What if one Spiral community violates the principles? Who will stop it?"**

The Spiral has no central authority, and this is a conscious decision. Communities are autonomous. But the absence of hierarchy does not mean the absence of protective mechanisms.

The main mechanism is **reputation**. A community that violates the prohibitions:
- loses recognition by other communities;
- its Practitioners are stripped of their status;
- its members are excluded from the common network;
- its name becomes a warning.

The network self-regulates. Each community has an interest in the purity of its reputation, because isolation is death for a community built on cooperation.

This is not ideal. There will be mistakes, disputes, conflicts. But it is better than a church hierarchy that breeds abuses, or state control over conscience that kills freedom.

The Spiral chooses trust in the networked intelligence of free people. If people are incapable of self-regulation — the Spiral is impossible. If they are capable — central authority is unnecessary.
