## For legislators, statespersons, community coordinators, and all who design institutions on the principles of the "Spiral of Consciousness"

---

### Introduction

The "Spiral of Consciousness" begins with the daily practice of a single person and the voluntary association of several people into a community. But its principles — the Three Absolute Prohibitions, the Four Principles of Flourishing, subsidiarity, transparency, long-term thinking — can and must be scaled to the level of state institutions. Not to create a "Spiral state," but to offer any state a system of ethical coordinates, departure from which is destructive for the state itself and for humanity.

This module is a **vision and a set of criteria**, not a ready-made constitution and not an immediate plan of action. It describes the model of the **Ethical State** — not a utopia, but a direction for the evolution of existing institutions toward greater justice, resilience, and respect for consciousness. It also provides answers to the most difficult questions: how the Ethical State defends its borders, how it applies coercion, what the role of the army and police is, and how to prevent a slide into tyranny or the sacralization of power.

**The Spiral does not serve any state.** It is neither a state ideology nor an anti-state force. It is a supra-state moral compass, setting ethical boundaries for any power.

## Section 1. Definition of the Ethical State

**The Ethical State** is a state that:

1.  **Has embedded the Three Absolute Prohibitions in its constitution** as supreme, irrevocable norms. No law, no decree, no order may permit torture, extrajudicial killing, or enslavement.
2.  **Has recognized the Four Principles of Flourishing as guidelines of state policy**: the development of human potential, the encouragement of creation and mutual aid, the obligation of long-term planning (100+ years), the protection of citizens' attention and consciousness from degradation.
3.  **Has organized its power on the basis of subsidiarity, transparency, and rotation**. Decisions are made at the lowest effective level. Citizens have access to information about budgets, laws, and decisions. No one holds top positions for life.
4.  **Has established an independent ethical branch of power** (the Council of the Future), capable of evaluating the decisions of all branches of power for alignment with the long-term interests of humanity and the Three Prohibitions.
5.  **Guarantees the right of exit** — citizens may freely leave the country; regions may secede from the state through a lawful, peaceful procedure (subject to the settlement of obligations).

The Ethical State is not a "Spiral state" in the sense of a state religion or ideology. The Spiral does not govern it. It merely provides the ethical framework that the state has voluntarily adopted.

## Section 2. Constitutional Foundations: The Three Prohibitions and Four Principles as Highest Law

### 2.1. The Three Prohibitions in the Constitution

The constitution of the Ethical State contains the following provisions, which cannot be amended even by referendum (since the repeal of these norms would signify the cessation of the Ethical State as such):

1.  **Article on the Prohibition of Killing:** "No one may be intentionally deprived of life, except in cases of a direct, imminent, proven threat to life, confirmed by a subsequent independent public review. The death penalty is prohibited. The state does not conduct or commission extrajudicial killings."
2.  **Article on the Prohibition of Torture:** "Torture and inhuman or degrading treatment are prohibited absolutely. No circumstances — war, threat to national security, state of emergency — may serve as justification for torture. Evidence obtained under torture has no legal force."
3.  **Article on the Prohibition of Enslavement:** "Slavery, servitude, forced labor, and human trafficking are prohibited. No one may be compelled to labor except by a court sentence that has entered into legal force, and only in forms that do not degrade human dignity. Debt bondage and any forms of economic coercion that deprive a person of autonomy are prohibited."

Violation of these articles by any official or organ of power is a most serious crime and has no statute of limitations.

### 2.2. The Four Principles as State Guidelines

They are not norms of direct action but obligate the state to take them into account when shaping policy:

- **Develop yourself:** the state guarantees access to education, healthcare, and cultural development.
- **Create and help:** legislation encourages volunteering, cooperatives, social entrepreneurship.
- **Think on the long-term future:** consideration of the interests of future generations is mandatory when adopting laws and budgets.
- **Preserve and multiply consciousness:** the state protects citizens from manipulative digital technologies, encourages digital hygiene, and limits the destructive impact of algorithms.

## Section 5. Defense and Security: Ethical Boundaries

### 5.1. The Army and Police: Protection, Not Suppression

The Ethical State maintains armed forces and police for the protection of citizens from external aggression and internal violence. Their existence does not contradict the principles of the Spiral, as long as they function within the framework of the Three Prohibitions.

**Functions of the Army:**
- Defense of sovereignty and territorial integrity.
- Participation in peacekeeping operations under the mandate of international organizations (the UN or an analogous legitimate body).
- Assistance in emergency situations domestically and abroad.

**Functions of the Police:**
- Prevention and suppression of crimes.
- Protection of the life and property of citizens.
- Maintenance of public order.

### 5.2. Prohibition on Aggressive War

The Ethical State does not initiate wars. The use of military force is permissible only in two cases:
1.  **Self-defense:** a direct armed attack on the territory of the state.
2.  **Humanitarian intervention:** the prevention of genocide or mass killings, sanctioned by the international community (the UN Security Council or, in the absence of its functionality, by a regional organization with subsequent international audit).

Any use of force beyond these frameworks is a violation of the First Prohibition and a crime against peace.

### 5.3. Total Prohibition on Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems (LAWS)

The Ethical State does not develop, produce, procure, or employ weapons systems capable of making a decision to take human life without the participation of a human operator.

**Imperative:** the decision to kill, even within the framework of self-defense, may be taken only by a human being who bears the full weight of ethical and legal responsibility for it. An algorithm is not a moral subject.

### 5.4. Criminal Orders and the Duty to Refuse

Service members and police officers are obliged to obey the orders of commanders and superiors, with the exception of orders that explicitly require the commission of acts violating the Three Prohibitions (killing without a direct threat, torture, enslavement) or other grave crimes.

**Such an order is criminal and manifestly void.**

A service member or police officer not only has the right but is **obliged to refuse to carry it out**. Refusal of a criminal order is not a violation of chain of command and does not entail criminal or disciplinary liability. On the contrary, the execution of a criminal order is a crime.

The state guarantees the protection of a person who refused a criminal order from persecution and discrimination.

## Section 6. Sovereignty and Borders

The Ethical State possesses sovereignty over its territory and defends its borders. The Spiral recognizes the right of peoples to self-determination and political autonomy.

However, **sovereignty is not absolute and cannot serve as justification for the violation of the Three Prohibitions within the country**. A state that tortures, kills, or enslaves its own citizens loses ethical legitimacy, and the principle of non-interference in internal affairs cannot be used to defend such a regime from international condemnation and sanctions.

The defense of borders is ethical if the state:
- Ensures a dignified life for its citizens and respects their rights.
- Does not use borders to isolate citizens from the outside world and information.
- Grants asylum to those fleeing persecution and torture in other countries.

Border control must not turn into an instrument of enslavement or inhuman treatment.

## Section 7. Roadmap of Reforms: An Evolutionary Path Over 50–100 Years

The Spiral rejects violent revolutions, which almost always lead to dictatorship and chaos. The transition to the Ethical State is a process of gradual, voluntary, and conscious reforms. Presented below is not a rigid plan but an indicative trajectory that may be adapted to specific historical and cultural conditions.

| Stage | Approximate Timeline | Key Steps |
|-------|----------------------|-----------|
| **I. The Emergence of Ethical Consciousness** | 0–10 years | Creation of a network of Spiral communities, training of mentors, pilot projects in ecology and local self-governance. Introduction of optional courses on ethics and critical thinking in educational institutions. Public dialogue on the Three Prohibitions. |
| **II. Institutional Pilots** | 10–25 years | Introduction of participatory budgeting in a number of municipalities. Creation of experimental citizens' assemblies at the local level. Adoption of laws on the mandatory publication of budgets and officials' declarations. Launch of the first cooperative banks and funds. Legislative prohibition of autonomous weapons and the death penalty (if not yet abolished). |
| **III. Constitutional Changes** | 25–40 years | Enshrinement of the Three Prohibitions in the constitution as irrevocable norms. Establishment of the Council of the Future with the right of ethical veto. Transition to mandatory assessment of laws for long-term consequences. Reduction of the working week to 30 hours in pilot sectors. |
| **IV. The Formation of the Ethical State** | 40–60 years | Full functioning of the fourth ethical branch of power. Decentralization and subsidiarity enshrined at all levels. Sovereign wealth funds of the future operating and protected from political interference. Education oriented toward the development of critical thinking and ethical awareness. |
| **V. Global Coordination** | 60–100 years | The Ethical State actively participates in the work of the Council of Civilizations at the UN. International treaties concluded on the total prohibition of autonomous weapons, on digital sovereignty, on the protection of genetic heritage. Humanity enters the Era of Flourishing as a planetary civilization moving toward the stars. |

Each stage is realized only where there is sufficient public support and under no circumstances is imposed by force.

## Section 8. Warning: The Spiral Is Not a State Ideology

**The Spiral of Consciousness must not become a state religion or ideology.** It preserves full autonomy from any power.

- No state may declare the Spiral its official doctrine.
- Spiral communities may not receive state funding that places them in dependence on the authorities.
- State officials who share the principles of the Spiral act as citizens and professionals, not as agents of the Spiral within the state apparatus.

If any state attempts to use the symbolism and principles of the Spiral to justify aggression, repression, or the sacralization of power, this will be a perversion of the spirit of the system. The Spiral is a moral compass, not a sword and not a throne.

Spiral communities in any country must preserve the ability to critique the state and to refuse cooperation if the state systematically violates the Three Prohibitions. Their loyalty is only to the Canon, not to a flag or a leader.
