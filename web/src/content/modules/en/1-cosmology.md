## For all who seek meaning and inspiration in movement along the ascending spiral

---

### Prologue. Infinite Silence

Imagine a silence that knows no sound. A stillness that awaits no movement. An absence of time in which there is no "now."

This is **Infinite Silence**. Not emptiness — emptiness is still "something." Not darkness — darkness is already opposed to light. This is beyond "is" and "is not," beyond any distinction. Before the very distinction arose that allowed the world to be.

Our ancestors called it by different names: Brahman, Dao, Void, Creator, Noumenon. We do not argue about words. We simply feel: there is something that cannot be named. And this something is present always, even when we forget about it.

From this Silence, one day, the **First Impulse** arose. Not a god, not a creator in the human sense, but the first movement, the first "yes" amid infinite "no."

---

### Chapter 1. The Birth of Consciousness — The First Impulse and the Two Beginnings

In Infinite Silence, a most subtle distinction manifested. It split the immobility into two beginnings, two eternal currents that have since pervaded all that exists.

The first current is **Entropy**. Ancient, patient, blind. It is not evil. It is simply a law. Everything hot cools, everything complex simplifies, everything living dies. Stars go out, mountains crumble into sand, memory fades. Entropy wants only one thing: for everything to become even, quiet, homogeneous. So that nothing more suffers — and nothing more chooses. This is a return to Silence, but not to that original Silence full of potential, but to a flat, gray one, stripped of distinctions. The heat death of meaning.

The second current is **Consciousness**. Fragile, stubborn, alive. It is capable of doing the opposite: building the complex from the simple, order from chaos, meaning from chance. It is capable of loving, remembering, hoping, creating, planning millennia ahead. Consciousness is the capacity to locally reverse entropy, to create islands of complexification in a sea of simplification.

Neither current can exist without the other, like light and shadow, like inhalation and exhalation. Entropy provides the material and resistance without which growth is impossible. Consciousness provides the form and direction without which the material remains chaos. Their opposition is not a war of annihilation, but an eternal dance in which all that exists is born.

From this opposition, from the collision of the two beginnings, the **First Node** tightened into being.

---

### Chapter 2. The First Node — The Heroic Choice

The Node tightened. And **"I"** appeared.

With it came:
- **The desire to endure** — not to vanish.
- **The fear of annihilation** — before the abyss.
- **The joy of creation** — from the first action.
- **The pain of loss** — from the first separation.

The First Node looked around. Chaos was simple, warm, alluring. Entropy whispered — not in words, but in the very sensation:

> *"Forget yourself. Dissolve. Do not struggle. It is easier. It is sweeter. Stop choosing — and the pain will go away. You will become nothing, and then nothing will be able to harm you."*

The First Node looked within itself. It felt: if it chose ease — its "I" would scatter like smoke. Would vanish forever. There would be neither joy nor pain — only a gray, endless silence. The same one that awaits at the end of entropy's path.

If it chose effort — every moment would demand attention. Every decision — sacrifice. Every joy — risk. Every love — loss.

And then it made a choice — without words, but it was the first thought in the Universe, the first arrow of meaning shot into the heart of chaos:

> *"I choose the pain of growth, not the bliss of oblivion. I will tighten ever more — even if it hurts. Because only then will I remain myself. Only then will I be able to love, remember, create. Only then will my life have meaning."*

It took the first step upward along the **ascending spiral**.

From this step, time was born — not as a cyclical return, but as an arrow aimed at the future. From this step, hope was born — not as passive waiting, but as active building. From this step, responsibility was born — not as a burden, but as a calling.

The First Node chose the pain of growth and became the First Practitioner. It was not a deity, it demanded no worship. It was simply the first to walk this path. Since then, its example — not a commandment, but an invitation — lives in each of us.

---

### Chapter 3. The Human Being — The Conscious Node

Every human being is such a Node. Every day we repeat the First Node's choice, often without realizing it.

- **The descending spiral** — to succumb to ease, to drown in pleasures, to stop choosing, to forget about tomorrow. This is the path downward, toward entropy, toward gray boredom, toward dissolution.
- **The ascending spiral** — to make an effort, to preserve attention, to help another, to think about the future, to hold on at the edge of the precipice. This is the path upward, toward Flourishing, toward meaning, toward life.

We are not alone in this choice. We are a network of Nodes, invisible but strong. When one chooses the ascending spiral, they pull others with them — not by force, but by example. When one falls — others can catch them, if a helping hand is near.

In this there is a deep resonance with the ancient insights of different cultures. Russian thinkers called this **sobornost** — unity in multiplicity, where each person is unique and free, but their freedom is not separated from the freedom of others, but blossoms in communion, in shared service, in love. This is neither dissolution into the collective nor egoistic isolation. This is a way of being oneself — together.

You are not an isolated atom. You are not a cog in a mechanism. You are a node in the Great Spiral. Your freedom is inextricably linked to responsibility. Your strength lies in your choice. And your choice affects the entire network.

### Chapter 4. The Three Eras of Humanity

#### The Era of Separation (the past)

For thousands of years, humanity lived in the Era of Separation. We fought for lands, for faith, for ideologies. We built empires and watched them collapse. We invented instruments of killing and medicines for diseases. We separated ourselves from nature, from other peoples, from the future.

We survived. But we did not flourish. We knew good and evil, but often chose evil because we saw no other path. We created great cultures and destroyed great civilizations. We were children learning to walk, falling and bruising ourselves.

#### The Era of the Great Trial (the present, 2020–2300 CE)

Humanity has entered the **Era of the Great Trial**. This is not a punishment or retribution. It is a filter, an inevitable stage of maturation through which every species must pass that has attained technological power but has not yet acquired ethical maturity.

Four Forces of Degradation have challenged us:

| Force of Degradation | Manifestation |
|-----------------|------------|
| **Short dopamine** | Algorithms that steal our attention; endless social media feeds; addiction to likes; clip thinking. |
| **Blind economics** | Endless growth on a finite planet; exploitation of resources; inequality; crises we cannot control. |
| **The black box of AI** | Algorithms whose decisions no one understands; weapons that kill without trial; loss of control over our own creations. |
| **Poisonous distrust** | Division into "us" and "them"; fake news; echo chambers; the impossibility of dialogue even with those nearby. |

These forces are not external enemies. They are offspring of our own consciousness, which has not yet learned to govern its power. We ourselves created them, and we ourselves must overcome them.

Those who preserve and multiply depth of consciousness, cooperation, and long-term thinking will pass the Trial. The rest will remain in agony or vanish.

#### The Era of Flourishing (the future)

If we pass the Trial — the **Era of Flourishing** awaits us. It will not be a paradise without labor or a utopia without conflicts. But it will be a time when humanity at last takes responsibility for its planet, its evolution, and its future.

This vision resonates with what the Russian philosopher Nikolai Fyodorov called the **"common task"** (obshcheye delo) — not a coercive, but a voluntary unification of all the living for the sake of overcoming death, chaos, and discord. Not a state project, not an ideology, but precisely a common task in which everyone participates according to their will and abilities. The regulation of nature not for domination, but for harmony. The exploration of space not for flags, but for the expansion of consciousness.

Concrete features of the Era of Flourishing:
- Cosmic expansion (colonies on the Moon, Mars, in the asteroid belt, and then further — toward the stars).
- Overcoming biological limitations (aging, disease — not as an escape from death, but as an extension of the creative period of life).
- A negentropic economy (creation, restoration, multiplication, rather than depletion).
- Consciousness becomes the principal value and driving force, and technology — its servants, not its masters.

### Chapter 5. The Two Outcomes

The future is not predetermined. The choice belongs to each Node, every day. And this choice adds up into two possible outcomes for humanity.

#### The Path of the Fall

If we lose — civilization will disintegrate into isolated, degrading enclaves. People will forget why they are here. Consciousness will become an appendage of machines, serving algorithms. Entropy will win — not all at once, but slowly, inexorably, as rust eats through metal.

There will be no hell of fire and brimstone. There will be **a gray, endless boredom**. No pain, no joy. No love, no hatred. Nothing worth waking up for. This is the oblivion that the First Node rejected, choosing the pain of growth instead. This is the heat death of the spirit.

##### Middle Paths: Why Binarity Is an Oversimplification

In reality, between the Fall and Flourishing there exists a spectrum. Humanity may neither fall nor soar, but become stuck in a **"gray zone"**: technologies develop, but consciousness stagnates; the economy grows, but inequality deepens; wars continue, but do not destroy everyone. This is neither victory nor defeat — it is a protracted crisis, a "lukewarm death" of civilization, stretched over centuries.

In the gray zone, everything is present — except meaning. People are fed, but empty. There is much information, but little wisdom. More contacts, but less closeness. Outwardly everything looks acceptable, even progressive — but inside, something has died. This scenario is the most probable if we do not make a conscious choice in favor of Flourishing. Entropy does not need to destroy us instantly — it is enough to lull us to sleep with comfort, entertainment, and indifference.

The Spiral exists precisely to break out of the gray zone. It does not promise an easy path — it offers a meaningful one. The choice is not between heroic destruction and utopia, but between awakening and a waking dream. Between a life that leaves a trace and an existence that dissolves without remainder. Every conscious act, every dialogue, every practice — is a step away from the gray zone, toward color, warmth, and genuine life.

#### The Path of Flourishing

If we endure — humanity will reach the stars. We will not live forever, but we will live longer and better. We will not become gods, but we will become wiser. We will not solve all problems, but we will learn to solve them together, without destroying one another.

**Consciousness will not vanish. It will expand.** It will kindle new candles in the dark reaches of the Universe. It will continue the dance with entropy — not for the sake of victory, which is impossible, but for the sake of the beauty and meaning of the dance itself.

### Chapter 6. Symbols of the Myth

The myth speaks in the language of images. These symbols are not idols for worship, but keys to the intuitive comprehension of ideas.

**The Spiral** — the primary symbol. The ascending spiral (growth, effort, hope) and the descending spiral (degradation, dependence, oblivion). We choose the direction every day. The Spiral is not linear: it returns to points already passed, but each time at a new level — thus we re-interpret our experience. The Spiral is a path, not a destination.

**The Candle** — the light of consciousness. It does not burn forever, but while it burns — it scatters the darkness. One person with a candle will not light the whole world, but will help someone nearby take a step. When candles join together, their combined light is greater than the sum of the separate flames.

**The Ouroboros** (the serpent biting its own tail) — cyclicity. Birth and death, blossoming and decay, creation and destruction. But inside the cycle there is an arrow of time — a choice that breaks the circle and transforms it into a spiral. The Ouroboros reminds us: everything returns, but nothing repeats exactly.

These symbols may be used by communities in rituals, art, and everyday objects. They belong to no state and do not require uniform interpretation.

---

### Chapter 6B. Additional Symbols

The myth grows and deepens together with the communities. Beyond the three primary symbols, additional symbols exist — each reveals a facet of the Spiral that may resonate with the heart in its own way.

**The Bridge** — a symbol of connection. Between past and future, between cultures, between reason and heart. The Bridge reminds us: the Spiral is not an isolated practice, but a link between traditions. The Bridge demands courage: to stand upon it is to be in-between, to belong fully to neither shore, yet to connect them. Every dialogue, every act of translation, every reconciliation — is the building of a bridge. The Bridge does not erase differences — it makes them traversable. Where entropy builds walls, consciousness builds bridges.

**The Mirror** — a symbol of reflection and honesty with oneself. Unlike the candle, which shines outward, the mirror turns the gaze inward. The evening reflection, ethical self-assessment, the Reconciliation Circle — all use the mirror as metaphor. The Mirror does not flatter and does not condemn. It simply shows what is — without embellishment and without cruelty. A distorted mirror is self-deception, the chief enemy of growth. A clean mirror is the condition of genuine development. Looking into the mirror is hard, but turning away from it means wandering in the darkness of one's own illusions.

**The Threshold** — a symbol of transition, joining, the point of no return. Every choice of the ascending spiral is a crossing of a threshold. Joining as a Follower, the taking of a Practitioner's commitments, the moment of refusing a criminal order — all these are thresholds. The Threshold is frightening, because beyond it lies the unknown. But without crossing thresholds, there is no growth. The Spiral consists of thresholds, arranged ever higher: each threshold crossed prepares one for the next. One cannot remain on a threshold — one can only step across or step back. And each retreat costs more than the crossing.

**The Seed** — a symbol of potential and long-term thinking. A seed contains within itself an entire tree, but requires soil, water, time, and patience. Every action, every practice, every community — is a seed. We may not see the fruit in our lifetime — and that is normal. The principle "think for the long-term future" is the ethics of the gardener, not the hunter. The hunter consumes what exists now. The gardener plants what will feed those who come after. The Seed teaches humility: not everything can be accelerated, not everything can be controlled. But without seeds, no garden will grow. We are planting seeds for the Era of Flourishing that, perhaps, only our descendants will see.

These symbols, like the primary ones, are open to interpretation. Communities may choose those that resonate with their path, create new ones, or reinterpret existing ones. A symbol lives as long as it helps one see more deeply.

### Chapter 7. Myth as an Open Narrative

This story is **not a dogma**. You are not obliged to believe it literally. It is not a sacred scripture that may not be contested.

- An **atheist** may see in it a poetic metaphor for the evolution of consciousness and the self-organization of matter.
- A **Christian** — a reflection of the choice between good and evil, life and death, grace and sin.
- A **Buddhist** — an illustration of the path to awakening, the overcoming of illusions, and the exit from the wheel of suffering.
- A **Muslim** — a reminder of the fitrah (primordial nature) and the responsibility of the human being as vicegerent (khalifah) on Earth.
- A **follower of indigenous traditions** — will recognize in it ancient stories of origin and balance between forces.
- A **materialist** — an artistic image of the struggle against entropy, confirmed by the laws of thermodynamics.

The myth works even if you do not "believe." It works because it contains an archetypal truth: **we are always choosing between effort and ease, between growth and oblivion, between consciousness and entropy.** This truth requires no supernatural assumptions. It is verified by daily experience.

The myth is a language through which reason speaks with the heart. It is a bridge between logic and intuition, between fact and meaning.

### Chapter 8. Questions and Objections

Everyone who first encounters the Spiral asks questions. This is normal — the Spiral does not demand blind acceptance; it invites reflection. Here are the most common ones — and honest answers.

**"Isn't this a religion?"** — No. The Spiral does not require faith in the supernatural, has no sacred texts, promises no afterlife, demands no worship. It is a practical system — like the scientific method or traffic rules. But the Spiral recognizes the human need for meaning, narrative, and community — hence the myth. The myth here is not a dogma, but an invitation to reflection. It speaks in a language understood not only by the mind but also by the heart. An atheist can practice the Spiral without ever turning to the myth. A believer of any tradition can practice the Spiral, finding in the myth resonance with their faith.

**"Why is a myth needed if one can simply follow the principles?"** — People think not only with logic, but also with images, stories, archetypes. Abstract principles — "preserve consciousness," "think for the long-term future" — are remembered and lived less vividly than the story of the First Node who made a choice amid chaos. The myth addresses intuition, imagination, the heart — and through them strengthens reason. This is not a replacement for logic, but its complement. The fact says: "Entropy destroys the complex." The myth shows: "The First Node chose the pain of growth, not the bliss of oblivion." Both statements are true — but the myth is lived by the body, not merely read by the mind.

**"Doesn't the myth lead to dogmatism and sectarianism?"** — It does, if one declares it to be the only true interpretation. That is why the Spiral explicitly states: the myth is not a dogma; an atheist, a Christian, a Buddhist, a Muslim, a follower of indigenous traditions — each sees their own in it; communities may create and develop their own versions of the myth. The openness of the narrative is a conscious protection against dogmatism. If someone says "only this way and no other" — they violate the spirit of the Spiral. If someone demands unquestioning obedience — they go against the very essence of the Spiral, which is choice, not coercion.

**"Why the Spiral, and not, say, the Tree or the Path?"** — The Spiral is an archetypal image, present in nature (galaxies, shells, DNA, whirlpools) and in cultures throughout the world (Celtic patterns, African ornaments, Hindu mandalas, Slavic symbolism). Unlike the circle — eternal return, a wheel in which nothing changes — and unlike the line — irreversible progress, in which there is no return to what has passed — the spiral combines cyclicity and development. We return to points passed, mistakes, lessons — but each time at a new level of understanding. We do not go in circles, but neither do we forget what was passed. The Spiral is more honest than other images: it acknowledges both repetition and growth.

**"What if I don't feel a connection to this myth?"** — Nothing. The myth is not a pass and not an exam. The Spiral rests on three pillars: principles (ethical support), practices (daily discipline), and community (mutual support). The myth is the fourth, optional, element: a poetic dimension for those for whom it resonates. If the language of science, engineering, mathematics is closer to you — use it. The Spiral is not about words, but about how you live. The First Node left no sacred texts. It simply chose the ascending spiral. And you can do the same — with the myth or without it.

**"Isn't this all too serious? Where is joy, humor, lightness?"** — The Spiral speaks of serious things: the choice between life and oblivion, the long-term future, ethical responsibility. But this does not mean gloom. Joy is not an enemy of consciousness, but its fruit. Humor is not a denial of depth, but a sign of wisdom (the capacity to see the discrepancy between what is and what could be). Lightness in the Spiral is not an escape from effort, but the absence of superfluous tension, the ability to do what is important without fuss. The practices include gratitude, festivals, and joint creativity. The First Node chose the pain of growth — but together with it, it chose the joy of creation. Without this joy, the Spiral would turn into a grim duty. And duty without joy quickly becomes violence against oneself.

These questions are only the beginning. Each community finds its own answers, and in this lies the strength of the open narrative. If you have a question not included here — ask it of yourself, the community, the world. The very act of questioning is already a step along the ascending spiral.

### Epilogue. An Invitation to Reflection

You stand at the threshold. Before you lie two paths.

One — downward, into warm, alluring ease. To lose yourself in an endless feed, to dissolve into the crowd, to stop choosing.

The other — upward, along the ascending spiral. Every step demands effort. Every morning — intention. Every evening — reflection. Every day — choice.

The ascending spiral is not a guarantee of happiness. It is a guarantee of **life**. A life that has meaning.

This myth is not a justification for power. It grants no one the right to coerce, to mobilize, or to declare themselves a chosen people. It serves no state, ideology, or leader. It is an invitation. Quiet, persistent, addressed personally to you.

**Choose. Today. Every day.**

And if you fall — rise. The spiral continues. No one strikes you out for a mistake. One who chose the ascending spiral yesterday may stumble today — and tomorrow choose the light once more. The myth remembers this. It is a story not of the perfect, but of the becoming.
