Ethics of The Path

How to apply the prohibitions and principles in daily life

The Three Prohibitions

1. Do Not Kill Intentionally

Exception: Direct, imminent, proven self-defense.

Rationale: Killing destroys all future cooperation with the victim, their loved ones, and witnesses. Creates cycles of revenge.

Requirements for exception:

  • Direct: Threat is present NOW
  • Imminent: Threat is about to happen NOW
  • Proven: Evident to any reasonable observer

Post-exception: There must be a public review.

2. Do Not Torture

No exceptions.

Rationale: Torture destroys victims, creates enemies, produces false information, and corrupts the torturer. Makes trust among all parties impossible.

What torture includes:

  • Physical pain intentionally inflicted
  • Psychological torment (isolation, threats)
  • Deliberate deprivation of basic needs

3. Do Not Enslave

No exceptions.

Rationale: Enslavement removes agency, creativity, and motivation. Creates resentment and rebellion.

What enslavement includes:

  • Forced labor under threat
  • Debt bondage
  • Human trafficking

Applying the Principles

Develop Yourself

Continuously increase your awareness, empathy, and responsibility. Grow through learning, reflection, and practice.

Create and Help

Add value to the world. Make things, solve problems, teach knowledge, support others.

Think Long-Term

Consider the consequences for future generations. Invest in lasting value.

Edge Cases

Not permitted. The threat is not direct and imminent — the person is already in custody.

Aggressive war is not permitted. Self-defense war requires the same criteria as individual self-defense: direct, imminent, proven threat.

Not permitted. The threat is not to life — only self-defense allows killing.
