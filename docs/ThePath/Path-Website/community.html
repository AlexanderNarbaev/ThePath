Community Charter

How communities organize, resolve conflicts, and grow together

Community Structure

Small Groups

2-10 people meeting regularly. The foundation of community life.

Local Communities

10-500 people in a geographic area. Self-governing with consensus.

Networks

Multiple communities working together on shared projects.

Decision-Making

  • Consensus-based: Seek agreement, not majority
  • Transparency: All decisions visible to members
  • Accountability: Clear roles and responsibilities
  • Exit right: Anyone can leave at any time

Conflict Resolution

  1. Direct dialogue: Parties try to resolve directly first
  2. Mediation: Neutral community member helps
  3. Arbitration: Panel of trusted members decides
  4. Restorative practices: Focus on healing, not punishment

How to Start a Community

  1. Find 2-10 people who share the Path's values
  2. Meet weekly for Assembly of Reason
  3. Practice the daily exercises together
  4. Establish decision-making by consensus
  5. Create projects that benefit your community
  6. Connect with other communities
