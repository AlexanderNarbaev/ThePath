About The Path

A minimal ethical framework for cooperation among all human beings

What Is The Path?

The Path is a minimal ethical framework designed to enable cooperation among all human beings, regardless of their religious beliefs, cultural background, political views, or philosophical worldview. It does not replace existing traditions — it provides an additional layer of agreement that allows people with fundamentally different views to work together toward common goals.

The central question the Path answers is not "What is the meaning of life?" or "What is the ultimate truth?" Instead, it answers a practical question: "What can we agree on, so that we can cooperate effectively?"

The Two Axioms

Axiom One

We exist as conscious beings in a world where our actions have consequences.

Our choices matter. What we do affects ourselves and others. This is the foundation of all ethics.

Every action has a reaction. Every choice creates consequences. This is not philosophy — it's observable reality.

Axiom Two

The future is open and is created by us through cooperation, science, and awareness.

We shape what comes next. Together, we can build a better future for all.

The future isn't fixed. We create it through working together, learning, and paying attention.

The Three Absolute Prohibitions

These are red lines that, if crossed, make long-term cooperation impossible.

❌ Do Not Kill Intentionally

Except direct, imminent, proven self-defense.

Killing destroys the possibility of cooperation with the person killed, their loved ones, and witnesses.

❌ Do Not Torture

No exceptions.

Torture destroys victims psychologically and physically, creates enemies, and produces false information.

❌ Do Not Enslave

No forced labor, debt bondage, or human trafficking.

Enslavement destroys agency, creativity, and motivation — making sustainable cooperation impossible.

The Three Positive Principles

These are desirable, not mandatory. They represent direction, not commands.

1. Develop Yourself

Continuously increase your awareness, empathy, and responsibility. Grow through learning, reflection, and practice.

2. Create and Help

Add value to the world. Make things, solve problems, teach knowledge, support others.

3. Think Long-Term

Consider the consequences of your actions for future generations. Invest in lasting value.

Who Can Accept The Path?

The Path is designed to be acceptable to:

  • Religious believers of all traditions (Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, etc.)
  • Atheists and agnostics
  • Secular humanists
  • People of all political persuasions
  • People of all cultural backgrounds

The only requirement is willingness to practice basic rational empathy — the ability to imagine oneself in another's position and act accordingly.

What The Path Is NOT

Not a Religion

No god, no afterlife, no holy texts, no required beliefs. It is a rational framework, not a faith system.

Not an Ideology

No political party, no mandatory program, no utopia promised. It enables cooperation, not compliance.

Not a Cult

No leader to worship, no money required, no secrets. It is open and transparent.

Not a Movement

No revolution, no violence, no overthrow of governments. It works within existing systems.
