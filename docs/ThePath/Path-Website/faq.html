The prohibition against killing does not apply to direct, imminent, proven threat to life — your own or another's. The threat must be: (1) direct (present NOW), (2) imminent (about to happen NOW), and (3) proven (evident to any reasonable observer). There must be a public review after any use of force.

Torture destroys trust — the foundation of all cooperation. Even if torture "worked" (it doesn't reliably produce accurate information), the prohibition stands because it destroys something essential: the trust that you will not be harmed by those with power.
