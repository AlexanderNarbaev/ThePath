# FAQ and Common Myths

> **Disclaimer:** This content is for academic, philosophical, and scientific research purposes only. It does not establish a religion, cult, or belief system. It is a minimal ethical framework for cooperation based on rational analysis, game theory, and evolutionary ethics. No supernatural claims are made. No worship, rituals with supernatural intent, or dogmatic faith is required. The framework is designed to be compatible with all existing religions and worldviews but does not replace them. All generated text is for scholarly discussion and ethical education.

## About the Path's Nature

### Q1: Is this a religion?

**A:** No. The Path has no god, no afterlife, no holy texts, no priests. It is a framework for cooperation grounded in reason and empathy. See Document 02 for foundations.

### Q2: Can I still be a Christian / Muslim / Buddhist?

**A:** Yes. The Path is compatible with most religions. It adds ethical guidance without replacing faith.

### Q3: Is this a cult?

**A:** No. The Path has no guru, no dogma, no secret knowledge, no financial demands. Membership is always voluntary. You may leave at any time.

### Q4: What about tradition?

**A:** The Path respects healthy traditions that support flourishing, while rejecting harmful ones that violate the prohibitions. See Document 02.

### Q5: What about technology?

**A:** The Path welcomes technology that supports the three principles (develop yourself, create and help, think long-term). It does not oppose innovation.

### Q6: What about science?

**A:** The Path supports science and does not contradict scientific consensus. It welcomes evidence-based reasoning.

## About Ethics and Prohibitions

### Q7: What about abortion?

**A:** The Path does not decide when life begins. It supports the woman's right to choose (bodily autonomy).

### Q8: What about the death penalty?

**A:** The Path recommends abolition. It violates the prohibition on killing except in immediate self-defense.

### Q9: What about assisted suicide?

**A:** Permitted. The Path supports the right to choose one's death with dignity.

### Q10: What about war?

**A:** Permitted only in defense, as a last resort, with discrimination between combatants and civilians, and with proportionality.

### Q11: What about torture?

**A:** prohibited absolutely.No exceptions.

### Q12: What about enslavement?

**A:** prohibited absolutely. No exceptions, except obligations arising from voluntary contracts.

### Q13: What about historical figures who violated the prohibitions?

**A:** We can learn from their contributions while condemning their violations. The Path separates the person from their actions.

### Q14: What about self-defense?

**A:** Permitted. The prohibitions do not apply to immediate self-defense against aggression.

### Q15: What about capital punishment for murderers?

**A:** The Path recommends abolition. Justice can be served through accountability without killing.

### Q16: What about genetic engineering or human enhancement?

**A:** Permitted if it does not violate the prohibitions and respects individual autonomy. Each person may choose their own body.

## About Politics and Economics

### Q17: What about politics?

**A:** The Path does not support any political party. It supports the prohibitions (no killing, torture, enslavement) in all systems.

### Q18: What about money?

**A:** The Path does not require money. All practices are free. Communities may use local currencies or mutual aid.

### Q19: What about poverty?

**A:** The Path recommends abundance for basic needs — food, shelter, healthcare, education. See Document 06.

### Q20: What about capitalism?

**A:** The Path does not prescribe an economic system. It requires that no one be enslaved and that long-term thinking guide decisions.

### Q21: What about population control?

**A:** The Path takes no position. Communities decide locally based on their resources and values.

### Q22: What about the right to bear arms?

**A:** Communities decide. Self-defense is permitted; aggressive violence is not.

### Q23: What about surveillance and data privacy?

**A:** The Path supports privacy and transparency. Communities create their own rules about data collection and access.

### Q24: What about inter-community conflict?

**A:** Mediated by neutral parties, or through peaceful exit. No coercion.

## About Daily Practice

### Q25: What about people who cannot practice daily reflection (e.g., cognitive impairment)?

**A:** Practices are optional and adaptable. Any method that supports self-reflection is welcome — the form matters less than the intent.

### Q26: What about mandatory vaccinations?

**A:** The Path recommends vaccination but does not mandate it. Education over coercion.

### Q27: What about free riders?

**A:** Communities address free riding through transparency and example, not coercion. See Document 05.

### Q28: What about prison?

**A:** The Path supports rehabilitation, not punishment. The goal is accountability and restoration, not suffering.

### Q29: What about censorship?

**A:** The Path opposes censorship. All speech is allowed unless it directly violates the prohibitions (e.g., incitement to violence).

## About Community and Relationships

### Q30: What about children refusing to participate?

**A:** Children are not forced to practice. They may participate in age-appropriate ways and choose fully when they reach the age of consent.

### Q31: What about children born into Path communities — are they automatically members?

**A:** No. Membership is always voluntary. Children reach full membership at the age of consent (typically 18).

### Q32: What about polygamy / polyamory?

**A:** Permitted as long as all parties consent. Consent is the governing principle.

### Q33: What about LGBTQ+ rights?

**A:** The Path affirms full equality. All persons deserve dignity and respect regardless of orientation or identity.

### Q34: What about race?

**A:** The Path is colorblind. All humans are equal in dignity.

### Q35: What about disability?

**A:** The Path requires accessibility in all practices. Communities must accommodate diverse abilities.

### Q36: What about refugees?

**A:** The Path recommends hospitality and exit rights. Anyone may leave a community and seek another.

### Q37: What about immigration between Path communities?

**A:** Free movement is encouraged. Communities may set reasonable limits for integration but may not trap members.

### Q38: What about the death of a member?

**A:** The community holds a Farewell ceremony to honor the person's contributions. See Document 04.

### Q39: What about non-human animals?

**A:** The Path focuses on human cooperation, but cruelty to animals is discouraged through the principle of empathy extension.

## About the Future

### Q40: What about AI rights?

**A:** The Path does not decide if AI has consciousness. It recommends caution: if an AI demonstrates consciousness, it may be considered a subject worthy of moral consideration.

### Q41: What about the use of AI in Path communities?

**A:** AI is a tool, like any technology. Communities may use it for efficiency, but decisions remain with humans.

### Q42: What about the environment?

**A:** The Path supports long-term sustainability. We have an obligation to future generations to preserve ecosystems.

### Q43: What about military service?

**A:** The Path does not forbid defense. It forbids aggression. Serving in defense of one's community is permitted.

### Q44: What about corruption?

**A:** Transparency prevents it. The Path requires open records, auditing, and public access to decisions.

### Q45: What about privacy?

**A:** The Path supports privacy. Not everything must be public — personal matters remain personal unless they harm others.

### Q46: What about the future of the Path itself?

**A:** The Path can evolve. The core (two axioms, three prohibitions, three principles) requires 90% global supermajority to change. Everything else may adapt. See Document 16.
