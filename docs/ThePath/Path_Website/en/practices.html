Practices and Rituals

Daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly practices that build awareness and connection.

Home › Practices

Why Practices Matter

While the axioms, prohibitions, and principles provide the intellectual framework, the practices provide the habitual structure that makes living according to the Path natural and automatic.

Practices are optional but highly recommended. They are not rituals in the religious sense — they are simple habits that develop awareness, accountability, and connection.

Level 1: Daily Practices (2 Minutes)

Morning Intention (1 minute)

Upon waking, before looking at your phone or starting your day:

  1. Pause and take one deep breath.
  2. Ask yourself three questions:
    • "What kind of person do I want to be today?"
    • "What will I do if I face anger / fear / conflict?"
    • "Whom can I help today?"
  3. Set your intention for the day.

Evening Reflection (1 minute)

Before sleeping:

  1. Pause and take one deep breath.
  2. Ask yourself three questions:
    • "What did I do well today?"
    • "What could I have done better?"
    • "Who did I help or harm today?"
  3. Let go of the day and rest.

Level 2: Weekly Practices

Assembly of Reason

A weekly gathering (60-90 minutes) for discussion, decision-making, and community bonding.

SegmentTimeDescription
Opening5 minBell or chime. Silence to gather attention.
Check-in10 minEach person says: "I am [name], and I feel [emotion]."
Topic30 minPre-announced topic — ethical dilemma, decision, guest speaker.
Open Discussion20 minAnyone can speak. No interruption. No judgment.
Closing5 minBell or chime. "We are [community]. Until next week."
FellowshipRemainingFood, conversation, connection.

Apophatic Silence

A period of silence (30-60 minutes) when the community meets without speaking. Simply be together — meditate, think, observe.

Level 3: Monthly Practices

Hour of Acknowledged Ignorance

A monthly practice dedicated to admitting what we do not know.

  1. Gather in a circle.
  2. Each person speaks one thing they do not know: "One thing I do not know is..."
  3. No solutions are offered. No advice is given.
  4. The group simply listens and witnesses.
  5. Close with: "We acknowledge our ignorance together."

Level 4: Yearly Festivals

Four main days per year, corresponding to solstices and equinoxes:

FestivalDateMeaning
Spring Equinox~March 20Renewal, cooperation, shared knowledge
Summer Solstice~June 21Peak energy, creation, growth
Autumn Equinox~September 22Harvest, reflection, education
Winter Solstice~December 21Darkness, memory, hope

Life-Transition Rituals

Optional rituals for major life transitions:

  • Birth: Welcoming — Welcome the newborn, promise to help them discover who they become.
  • Coming of Age: Awakening — Around 12-15 years. "You are now responsible for your choices."
  • Marriage/Partnership: Union — "You choose each other. You will support each other's growth."
  • Death: Farewell — "[Name] lived. [Name] contributed. [Name] is honored."
