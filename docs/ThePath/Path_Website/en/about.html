What Is the Path?

The Path is a minimal ethical framework designed to enable cooperation among all human beings, regardless of their religious beliefs, cultural background, political views, or philosophical worldview. It does not replace existing traditions, religions, or personal belief systems — it provides an additional layer of agreement that allows people with fundamentally different views to work together toward common goals.

The central question the Path answers is not "What is the meaning of life?" or "What is the ultimate truth?" Instead, it answers a much more practical question: "What can we agree on, so that we can cooperate effectively?"

The Open Spiral represents growth, continuous development, and forward movement without a final endpoint. Unlike a closed circle (which implies completion), the spiral has no closure — it keeps expanding outward, symbolizing the open-ended nature of human potential and the ever-improving future we create together.

Two Axioms

Axiom 1: Actions Have Consequences

We exist as conscious beings in a world where our actions have consequences.

All human beings experience themselves as existing — as subjects who perceive, feel, think, and choose. This experience of selfhood is inescapable. Furthermore, the world responds to our actions in predictable ways: pushing an object causes it to move, speaking kindly strengthens relationships, lying damages trust, harming others creates enemies.

Why this matters:

  • Ethics becomes rational. If actions have consequences, then some actions produce suffering while others produce flourishing.
  • Rights become practical necessities. If I harm you, you will likely retaliate, withdraw cooperation, or suffer.
  • Justice becomes possible. Without consequences, there is no justice — only power.

Axiom 2: The Future Is Open

The future is open and is created by us through cooperation, science, and awareness.

The future has not been fixed in advance by any deity, fate, or physical law. While certain outcomes are more probable given certain actions, no outcome is absolutely determined. We create the future through three engines of progress:

  • Cooperation: Collective action achieves what individuals cannot
  • Science: Systematic inquiry reveals what works and doesn't work
  • Awareness: Mindfulness of consequences allows us to correct course

Three Prohibitions

These are rational red lines — actions that, if crossed, make long-term cooperation impossible.

Prohibition Description Why
Do not kill Except direct, imminent, proven self-defense Killing destroys all possibility of future cooperation
Do not torture No exceptions Torture destroys trust — essential to cooperation
Do not enslave No forced labor, debt bondage, human trafficking Enslavement destroys agency and creativity

Three Principles

These are desirable, not mandatory. They represent the direction toward which we should move.

1. Develop Yourself

Continuously increase your awareness, empathy, and responsibility. Grow as a person through learning, reflection, and practice.

2. Create and Help

Add value to the world. Make things, solve problems, teach knowledge, support others — according to your abilities and opportunities.

3. Think Long-Term

Consider the consequences of your actions for future generations. Invest in things that will benefit your children, grandchildren, and beyond.

What the Path Is NOT

  • Not a religion — No god, no afterlife, no holy texts, no required beliefs
  • Not an ideology — No political party, no mandatory program, no utopia promised
  • Not a cult — No leader to worship, no money required, no secrets
  • Not a movement — No revolution, no violence, no overthrow of governments

Who Can Accept the Path?

The Path is designed to be acceptable to:

  • Religious believers of all traditions (Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, etc.)
  • Atheists and agnostics
  • Secular humanists
  • People of all political persuasions
  • People of all cultural backgrounds

The only requirement is willingness to practice basic rational empathy — the ability to imagine oneself in another's position and act accordingly.
